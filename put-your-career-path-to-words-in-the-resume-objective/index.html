Put your Career Path to Words in the Resume Objective

MakeMyResume

With growing job demands, resume writing trends have changed. Thus, adding a resume objective is essential, especially when you have less than 5 years experience. It states your career goals. It is as simple as a Resume Headline but gives an idea to the hiring manager of how you see yourself growing in your career.

A resume objective thus focuses on your skills and abilities and what makes you different from the rest. A well-defined objective convinces your hiring manager about your competencies. It’s not difficult to develop a good one. However, you have to put in a lot of thought before you actually come up with an ideal one.

What a Resume Objective is supposed to say?

Straightforwardly, a Resume Objective is a short blurb telling the hiring manager what skills and abilities you possess as an individual. Consequently, the manager is convinced that the set of skills and abilities will help his organization achieve its goals. Thus, your Resume Objective needs to be tailor-made. It can also speak about any accomplishments you wish to achieve for the company.

What a Resume Objective is not supposed to say?

A Resume Objective is not supposed to state what you want from the company. In addition, it is not supposed to state how it will help you achieve your own goals. Moreover, it is meant to focus on the bigger picture rather individual goals. Uniquely include ways you can add more value to the company.

For Eg: Mention your ten years of successfully reducing budgets and state that you want to apply these skills to the organization's budget.

What makes a good Resume Objective?

A good Resume Objective is a perfect blend of a candidates learning experience and relevant skills. Thus, draft one in a manner that showcases proven mastery in previous job roles.

For Eg: Search engine optimization position where I can use my SEO skills and experience to increase site traffic and search engine placement, and apply my 4 years of IT experience.

Furthermore, include communication and sales skills relevant to your job role. Correspondingly, expand on your achievements and experience that prove to have these skills.

For Eg:  Seeking a position as a clinical practice assistant for a health maintenance organization, utilizing my award-winning writing, research, and leadership skills.

Your focus must be to gain a leverage among the other candidates who apply for a similar job. Thus, the key to crafting a good Objective is focusing on your skills, experience, and achievements. A good resume can give you an edge over other candidates - so good ahead and craft your Resume Objective!
